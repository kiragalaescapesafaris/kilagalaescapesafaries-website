UGANDA - The Pearl of Africa

Uganda is a landlocked country located in the EAstern part of Africa astride the Equator, bordered by South Sudan to the north, Kenya to the east, Tanzania to the south, the Democratic Republic of Congo to the west, and Rwanda to the southwest. Uganda is one of the best tourist destinations in Africa with a variety to offer ranging from wildlife and plant life, geography, Landscape, Rich culture and tradition, climate, soils, drainage, relief, plateaus, natural beauty, unique magnificent features and creatures, plus its very accommodative hospitable local people. These rich attributes are the reason as to why the country was referred to as the “Pearl of Africa” by Sir Winston Churchill in his book My African Journey written in 1908 after his tour to Uganda. Churchill concluded by declaring the country the Pearl of Africa, emphasizing Uganda’s eye-catching colors, bird life, reptiles, insects, beasts, mammals, vegetation, and climate. On a Uganda safari adventure, tourists are amazed by the country’s stunning features and often confirm that Uganda truly deserves the title the Pearl of Africa. Uganda is the source of the longest river in the world, the Nile River. The country shares Africa’s largest lake with Kenya and Tanzania, Lake Victoria occupying 45% of it. The county famously known for being home to more than 50% of the remaining endangered mountain gorillas in the World.Uganda is also home to the third highest point in Africa, Mountain Rwenzori—known as the “mountain of the moon”. Uganda is famous for hosting the iconic number of African Big Five, it's as well home to debatably the second-deepest lake in Africa Lake Bunyonyi.

It boasts unique wildlife such as tree-climbing lions in Queen Elizabeth National Park. Uganda is one of the dew counties where visitors can stand with one leg in the northern hemisphere and the other in the southern hemisphere at the Equator. Murchison Falls National Park, featuring the magnificent Murchison Falls” world’s most powerful Falls”, was Uganda’s first and largest National Park. The country is also the home to the Rothschild’s giraffes endemic to only Uganda and Kenya—as well as chimpanzees in Kibale Forest National Park aslo known as “ the Primate Capital of Africa” Uganda is truly the Pearl of Africa, a land of great diversity, beauty, and opportunities. It is a country on the move with young educated people. English is commonly used in the central region for it is the country’s official language, while Luganda is mostly spoken in central Uganda because central is the home for the Baganda people. Other parts of the country use English to varying degrees, alongside their local languages. Ugandans are very welcoming and accommodative people. Uganda lies in the African Great Lakes region. Most of the country is situated on a plateau marked by mountains and valleys. Kampala is the largest and Capital city of Uganda, Kampala is a very lively and chaotic city due to its dense population — with most of the country’s business conducted here. The city is famously built on seven hills. Kampala city got its name from “empala,” the plural of impala, a species of antelope that used to populate these Seven hills. Kampala means the hills of empala. The country’s name, Uganda, originates from the Buganda kingdom, which covers a large portion of the southern part of the country.

UGANDA’S REGION AND PEOPLE

As of 2023, Uganda’s population is around 49.6 million people, with about 8.5 million residing in Kampala. Uganda is a multicultural country with over 56 tribes distributed in different regions, the Central region is predominantly inhabited by the Baganda tribe but also hosts other groups so generally every tribe can be found in the Central. The Northern region has the Alur, Acholi, Jopadhola, Langi, Madi, Lugbara, Okebu, and Metu tribes. The Eastern region is home to the Basoga, Bagishu, Basamia, Karamojongs, Itesots, Karam, Sebei, Pokot, Labwor, Tepeth, Kakwa, among other tribes. The Western region includes the Batoora, Bagwere, Banyolo, Bakonjo, Banyankole, among others. Uganda is truly blessed with each region boasting a unique tourist attraction: the Western region hosts 90% of Uganda’s National Parks; the Northern region offers a rich, untouched African cultural despite the fast changing world, the Central region is the country’s industrial hub and capital; and the Eastern region features the source of the world’s longest river and home to the adventure capital of East Africa, Jinja. Uganda, often called the “Pearl of Africa,” is renowned for its lush green landscapes and incredible wildlife diversity. From vast savannahs teeming with elephants and lions to dense tropical forests home to chimpanzees and rare endangered mountain gorillas, Uganda offers a spectacular setting for every nature lover and traveller.
