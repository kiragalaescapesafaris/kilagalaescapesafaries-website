Tourist Attractions in Uganda

Jinja and Lake Victoria (Source of the Nile)

Jinja is a renowned tourist destination in East Africa also referred to as the adventure capital of East Africa, famous for being home to the source of the Nile River (White Nile). The Nile flows from Lake Victoria to the Mediterranean Sea, making it the longest river in the world. Jinja serves as a great starting and ending point for your Uganda safari adventure with a variety of a must try water activities and some include rafting, kayaking, boat tours and other activities include bird watching, Cultural tours, visiting the Speke monument at the source of the Nile, and walking across the wooden deck towards the river's origin.

Sipi Falls (Hiker’s Paradise)

Sipi Falls is one of the most scenic destinations you shouldn't miss out while on your Uganda safari Adventure. Sipi is located near the Uganda-Kenya border sitting on a spectacular, pristine green landscape accessible only by foot. The area is known for its Arabica coffee plantations. Sipi boasts three waterfalls that tourists can hike on a 7-8 km guided tour with a local expert knowledgeable about the history, geography and trails of the falls. Visitors can also tour coffee plantations, experiencing the coffee process first hand from planting to processing and stand a chance to on fresh and natural coffee straight from the source.

Kidepo National Park

Kidepo National Park, the most isolated and least visited of Uganda’s national parks, lies in the northern part of the country. It is considered a natural gem, hosting over 77 wildlife species such as leopards, wild dogs, cheetahs, lions, elephants, and giraffes. The park is often referred to as Uganda’s Serengeti. Kidepo was ranked highly by CNN Travels because of its spectacular landscape and great buffalo herds numbers as the third best park in Africa in 2013.

Murchison Falls National Park

Murchison Falls National Park is Uganda’s largest and oldest National park, also one of the most visited. Its major attraction is the mighty thunderous Murchison Falls, where water plunges through a narrow gorge generating an incredible roar that shakes the earth beneath making it the world’s powerful waterfalls. The park is home to the African Big Five (excluding rhinos), leopards, hippos, crocodiles, and a rich birdlife. Activities include game drives, boat cruises, nature walks, bird watching among others.

Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary

The only place to see rhinos in Uganda, the most rewarding is that the sanctuary offers tourists a chance to trek these animals on foot giving you a good and close encounter with them.The Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary protects these endangered animals from poachers. Located near Murchison Falls National Park, visitors can take guided walking treks to see rhinos and enjoy other activities like shoebill bird tracking, night walks, and canoe rides.

Igongo Cultural Centre, Mbarara

Opened in 2011, the Igongo Cultural Centre preserves the rich cultural heritage of Uganda’s western people of Uganda. On your way to Queen Elizabeth National Park, Mgahinga Gorilla National park or Bwindi Impenetrable National Parks, you can stop here to enjoy the pre-colonial tradition of Africa, local meals, performances, explore African huts decorated with local crafts, stroll through gardens with beautiful trees and sculptures, and shop for memorable souvenirs from the Pearl of Africa for your loved ones back home.

Lake Bunyonyi

Lake Bunyonyi is arguably Africa’s second deepest lake, it is a freshwater body free of bilharzia and crocodiles, making it safe for swimming. Lake Bunyonyi has over 29 islands including the historical Punishment Island. The lake was named after the many small birds that inhabit it, the word Bunyonyi locally means small birds. It is a perfect spot for relaxation, bird lovers and offers various activities like boat cruises, canoeing, birdwatching, swimming, horse-riding, island visits, and cultural tours.

Gorilla Trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable and Mgahinga National Parks

Uganda hosts the largest population of the remaining endangered mountain gorillas. These endangered Apes carry 98% of human DNA. The gorillas are habituated and safe to visit while following guidelines and rules. Trekking mountain gorillas is a once in a life lifetime and unforgettable experience, it's a highlight of any Uganda safari. Bwindi’s dense forest requires daily maintenance of trekking trails and rainy conditions, earning its reputation as a true African jungle because of its dense impenetrable vegetation cover.

Queen Elizabeth National Park

Queen Elizabeth is Uganda’s most famous and frequently visited National park, Queen Elizabeth National Park is home to more than 96 mammal species, including the unique tree-climbing lions found in its Ishasha sector. Visitors also get a chance to sight on other animals like hippos, crocodiles, elephants, warthogs, primates, and a vast variety of bird species, surrounded by rich vegetation and ecosystems.

Nyero Rock Paintings

Located in eastern Uganda in Kumi district, Nyero Rock Paintings are among Uganda’s most important ancient artworks, believed to date before 1250 CE. These stone-age paintings depict animals like zebras and are spread across these rocks, each with unique patterns offering different experiences and mysteries concerning their creators.

Equator Sign Kayabwe

Uganda is one of the few countries crossed by the Equator, dividing the world into northern and southern hemispheres. The Kayabwe Equator landmark attracts nearly every visitor to Uganda’s western region and serves as a fun photographic spot where one can stand with a leg in each hemisphere. Uganda recently unveiled a new equator monument at Kikorongo in Kasese district.
